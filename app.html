Interactive: Visualizing the 82nd Legislative Session

  • by Becca Aaronson and Ben Hasson
  • June WOOT!, 2011

Lawmakers had two big budget problems to solve this session — a $4 billion deficit in the current biennium budget and planning for the 2012-2013 biennium with an estimated $15 billion revenue shortfall. It was a long and confusing process for most, but by the end of the regular session the House and Senate had passed bills to solve the deficit and a budget bill for next biennium. So why are they still at the Capitol battling out how to balance the budget during a special session?

We’ve culled The Texas Tribune’s coverage of the budget shortfall and created an interactive time line to link readers back to the major budget developments before and during the regular session.

The graphic is color-coded to help readability. Blue stories cover developments in the budget process inside the legislature. Stories specifically related to the Rainy Day Fund are in turquoise. Red stories cover developments outside the lege — like the comptroller’s announcements and The Texas Tribune and University of Texas’ insider polls. There are also a few tidbits in gold from Governor Rick Perry. If you get confused along the way, the green stories provide a clear update of where everything stands. Also look out for the LiveBlog, TribLive and multimedia icons, which indicate special content is available inside the story.
